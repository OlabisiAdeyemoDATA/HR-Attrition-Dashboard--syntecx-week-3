# HR-Attrition-Dashboard--syntecx-week-3
This project analyzes employee attrition using the Atlas Labs HR dataset to uncover workforce trends, identify key drivers of employee turnover, and provide actionable recommendations for HR decision-makers.
The dashboard is designed using a storytelling approach, answering four key business questions:

What is happening?
Where is it happening?
Why is it happening?
What should HR do about it?               

Business Objectives
Analyze employee attrition across the organization.
Identify departments with the highest turnover.
Explore relationships between salary, experience, and employee attrition.
Perform correlation analysis to identify the strongest drivers of attrition.
Provide actionable HR recommendations to improve employee retention.  

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Total Employees
Employees Left
Attrition Rate
Retention Rate
Average Salary
Average Years at Company

Dashboard Features
Employee Attrition Overview
Department-Level Attrition Analysis
Overtime vs Attrition Comparison
Salary vs Years at Company Scatter Plot
Correlation Analysis of Attrition Drivers
HR Action Plan with strategic recommendations
Interactive Department and State slicers

Key Insights
The organization has an attrition rate of 16.1%, with 237 employees leaving out of 1,470 employees.
Promotion opportunities show the strongest relationship with employee attrition.
Employees with shorter tenure are more likely to leave.
Overtime is associated with higher attrition.
Salary alone has a relatively weak relationship with employee attrition.

HR Recommendations
Improve promotion and career development opportunities.
Strengthen manager-employee relationships.
Implement retention programs for early-career employees.
Reduce excessive overtime through better workforce planning.
